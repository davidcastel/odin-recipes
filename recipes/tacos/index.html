Home-style Tacos al Pastor (Chile and Pineapple Pork Tacos)

Description

Quoted by the article:

Tacos al Pastor are a favorite Mexico and Mexican restaurants throughout the world for good reason: The mixture of smoky, spicy chiles, sweet pineapples and fresh onions and cilantro is taco perfection.
Traditionally, Tacos al Pastor are made by marinating pork in chile sauce, layering the meat on a vertical rotisserie, adorning with a pineapple and roasting slowly for hours--a process that's almost impossible to replicate at home.
So we've take the delicious, authentic flavors that make these tacos so special, and adapted the recipe for your home kitchen, so you can enjoy it whenever a craving strikes!

For the pork

  • 5 Goya Guajillo Chiles
  • 5 Goya Pasilla Chiles or Goya Ancho Chiles
  • 1 chipotle pepper from a can of Goya Chipotle Chile in Adobo Sauce
  • 1 medium white onion, halved
  • 1 (20 ounce) can Goya Pineapple Chunks
  • 1/4 cup Gota White Vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons Goya Minced Garlic
  • 1 teaspoon Goya Cumin
  • Goya Adobo with Pepper, to taste
  • 1 (2 1/2 pound) boneliness, skinless prok butt, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons Goya Vegetable Oil

For the Garnsih

  • 1 (10 ounce) package Goya Corn Tortillas, warmed
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Step 1:

    Bring 2 cups water to boil in medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add guajillo and passilla chiles. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until chiles soften, about 10 minutes; transfer to plate. Remove and discard stem and seeds. Meanwhile, coarsely chop one onion half; reserve remaining half. Strain pineapples; reserver juice and fruit separately.

  2. Step 2:

    Transfer guajillo, pasilla and chipotle chiles, choppedonion half, reserved pineapple juice, vinegar, garlic and cumin to bowl of food processor. Pure until smooth, about 2 minutes. Trasnfer chile mixture to saucepan over medium-high heat. Bring chile mixture to a boil; cook until paste loses raw onion taste, about 2 minutes. Season with Adobo; cool. In large contianer with lid, or in large ziptop bag, combine pork cubes, cooled chile marinade and reserved pineapple chunks; transfer to refrigerator. Marinate at least 2 hours, or up to 24 hours.

  3. Step 3:

    Heat oil in large skillet over medium-high heat. Strain pork and pineapples, discarding marinade. Add pork pineapples to skillet. Cook in batches until dark golden brown on all sides and cooked trhough, about 15 minutes; transfer to large serving plate.

  4. Step 4:

    Meanwhile, finely slice remaining onion half. Trasnfer sliced onion to bowl with cilantro. Serve pork and pineapple mixture in warm torillas. Garnish pork tacos with cilantro, onions and limes.
